Privacy Guide

This page outlines practical steps to enhance your privacy when using no‑KYC platforms. Nothing here is financial or legal advice.

Core principle: Minimize linkability between your real identity, devices, and on‑chain activity.

Account & OPSEC

  • Use unique emails per service via aliases (e.g., SimpleLogin, Addy.io). Avoid reusing identifiers.
  • Generate strong passwords with a local manager (KeePassXC, 1Password) and enable TOTP where available.
  • Segment personas: separate browser profiles for trading, research, and social accounts.

Network Privacy

  • Prefer Tor for P2P and privacy-critical actions; otherwise use a reputable, no‑logs VPN with killswitch.
  • Harden your browser: disable third‑party cookies, block fingerprinting, and use privacy‑focused extensions only when needed.

Wallet Hygiene

  • Use fresh receive addresses; avoid reusing addresses across services.
  • Separate “identity-tainted” coins from “clean” coins across different wallets.
  • Consider hardware wallets for cold storage; verify firmware and supply chain.

On‑Chain Practices

  • Understand that most L1s are transparent; consider privacy techniques appropriate to the chain.
  • For Bitcoin, learn UTXO management, coin control, and avoid address reuse.
  • If you use cross‑chain bridges, verify contract addresses and recent audits. Start with small test amounts.

Device Security

  • Keep OS and browser updated. Enable full‑disk encryption and secure boot.
  • Beware clipboard hijacking and fake wallet extensions. Verify downloads and signatures where possible.

Buying & Cashing Out

  • When using P2P, follow escrow instructions strictly and keep communication within the platform.
  • For ATMs, compare fees and limits beforehand; avoid shoulder surfing and keep receipts offline.

Common Mistakes

  • Reusing emails, phone numbers, or IPs across multiple platforms.
  • Mixing custodial and non‑custodial funds without tracking provenance.
  • Clicking affiliate links from unknown sites; always verify destination domains.
Disclaimer: Privacy is a spectrum. Regulations vary by country. Ensure your actions comply with local laws.
